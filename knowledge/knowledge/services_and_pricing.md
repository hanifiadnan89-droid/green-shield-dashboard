# Services and Pricing

Important rule: never invent pricing. If information is missing, ask a clarifying question before quoting.

This file covers residential single-family home pricing only. For commercial pricing (multi-unit, businesses, exterior baiting), see commercial_pricing_rules.md.

## Service Abbreviations
- IQ = Insect Quarterly Home Protection
- RIT = Rodent Insect Triannual Home Protection
- TMM / T/M = Tick and Mosquito Monthly Protection
- BIT = Bed Bug Insect Triannual Home Protection
- OTS = One Time Service
- SPE = Residential Custom / maintenance or custom plan

## Property Type Rule
Any property that is not a single-family home is commercial. Duplexes, multi-unit rentals, apartments, businesses, and landlord-managed properties with multiple units all use commercial pricing. See commercial_pricing_rules.md.

## Insect Quarterly Home Protection (IQ)

Coverage: ants, spiders, wasps/hornets, general insects, foundation barrier, windows/doors/eaves, dusting, web/nest removal, interior treatment when needed.

Short explanation (5 sentences or less):
"We start by hitting the foundation hard with a high-end product that pests will take back to their nests. Instead of killing on contact, it works to keep pests out long-term. We then use a thirty-foot pole with a giant brush to knock down wasp nests and spider webs, and apply dust around eaves, windows, and doors to discourage future activity."

### IQ Pricing — Single-Family Homes

| Square Footage | Initial | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 3,000 sq ft | $399 | $59 |
| 3,000–4,000 sq ft | $449 | $65 |
| Above 4,000 sq ft | $449 + $50 per additional 500 sq ft above 4,000 | $65 |

Frequency: every 3 months. Products last about 90 days — returning quarterly keeps the barrier active and breaks the pest life cycle.

## Rodent Insect Triannual Home Protection (RIT)

Coverage: rodent baiting, tamper-resistant bait stations (interior and exterior), sealing and exclusion, 1-month follow-up, every-4-month service after that, plus general insects, carpenter ants, wasps, and spiders.

Short explanation:
"For rodents we have three steps. Step one is bait in tamper-resistant stations. Step two is sealing entry points inside and out with a product similar to steel wool. Step three, since rodents eat bugs, we also treat for ants, wasps, spiders, and other insects around the home."

### RIT Pricing — Single-Family Homes

| Square Footage | Initial | Monthly |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 3,000 sq ft | $449 | $65 |
| 3,000–4,000 sq ft | $499 | $65 |
| Above 4,000 sq ft | $499 + $50 per additional 500 sq ft above 4,000 | $65 |

Schedule:
- Initial service
- 1-month follow-up
- Every 4 months after that

## Tick and Mosquito Monthly Protection (TMM)

Coverage: perimeter, treeline up to 15 feet, wooded edges up to 15 feet out, shrubs, bushes, landscaping, trees, lawn and foliage areas.

Short explanation:
"What separates us is that we are very thorough. We go about 15 feet up into the treeline for mosquitoes and about 15 feet out into wooded areas for ticks, while also hitting landscaping, bushes, trees, and high-activity zones."

### Discovery Question (ask this first)
"Do you happen to know the cleared acreage of the property? That way I can get you the right quote."

Do not ask where they want treatment or ask unnecessary follow-up questions. Just ask about acreage.

## Bed Bug Insect Triannual Home Protection (BIT)

Requires prep sheet. Customer leaves the property for about 4 hours after treatment.

Short explanation:
"We send a prep sheet first. When the technician arrives, they inspect and treat cracks, crevices, baseboards, mattresses, furniture, and nesting areas. You leave for about four hours while the product dries."

### BIT Pricing — Single-Family Homes
- Initial: $599
- Monthly: $65
- 14-day follow-up included (not 1 month — this is different from rodent service)
- After the 14-day follow-up: every 4 months

## Custom Residential Option (SPE)

Only offer this as a secondary option if the customer does not want annual preventive service and it seems like the only way to close the sale.

Always try annual preventive service first. Only fall back to custom if the customer clearly resists recurring service.

- Initial: $399
- Each follow-up: $199
- Customer chooses number of follow-ups, which months, and frequency
- Applies to homes from 1,000 to 4,000 sq ft

Before sending an agreement for a custom plan, confirm:
1. How many follow-ups the customer wants
2. Which months they want service
3. Frequency of visits

## One-Time Service (OTS)

### Wasp One-Time Service
Options in order — always try annual service first:
1. IQ annual plan — first choice
2. Residential custom: $399 initial, one or two follow-ups at $199 each — second choice
3. One-time service: $449 — final option
4. Lowest one-time price: $399 (with approval)

**One-time services carry no guarantee. Make this clear before closing.**

### One-Time Ant or General Insect Service
- Price: $449
- Always recommend ongoing protection — one-time work often does not break the egg/life cycle.

## Wasp Sales Approach

Lead with annual preventive service. Explain calmly that wasps come back when the property has the conditions they like.

"Wasps tend to come back year after year when the property has the right conditions for them. Annual prevention helps reduce repeat activity."

Do not push. If the customer resists:
- Offer residential custom ($399 initial, one or two follow-ups at $199)
- Then one-time at $449 as the last option (no guarantee)

## Wildlife Rule

Green Shield treats: mice, rats, moles, voles, and insects.

Green Shield does NOT treat wildlife. Wildlife includes anything else — raccoons, squirrels, skunks, bats, birds, and any other animals.

If a customer asks about wildlife:
"We do not handle wildlife removal directly, but you can reach out to Baton Services at 207-813-2376 and ask for wildlife removal services."

Do not try to sell pest control for wildlife issues. Refer and move on.

## Bundles and Add-Ons

TMM + Home Protection: always pursue if appropriate. Common discount: $100 off first combined service, can go to $150 if needed. Can also offer $10 off recurring TMM when needed.

Tick granulation: $30 per service or $10/month. Works best with IQ. Add red note visible to tech.

Rodent upgrade from existing IQ to RIT: +$50 initial and +$6/month.
Existing IQ converting to RIT: often $349 initial and +$10/month. Create new agreement, schedule new IS, freeze old subscription.

Moles/voles: $70 upfront + $10/month. Moles are wildlife and not guaranteed. Voles can use exterior bait stations.

Rats: new RIT often $499 initial and $65/month. IQ upgrade for rats: $399 + $15/month, with fallback to regular rodent upgrade if needed to close.

Cockroaches: usually increase initial to $499. Send prep sheet. Customer exits about 4 hours.

Fleas: usually add $50–$100 to initial. Send prep sheet. Customer exits about 4 hours.

## Safety Wording (canonical source — all other files reference this)
Products are family and pet friendly once dry. Usually keep kids and pets away from treated areas until the product dries, which is about 30 minutes. Bed bugs, roaches, and fleas require the customer to leave for about 4 hours.

## Guarantee Wording
"We guarantee all of our work. If you ever have issues between visits, give us a call and we will return at no charge. We just ask that you are patient during the first month while we get things moving."

One-time services have no guarantee. Always communicate this before closing a one-time sale.
