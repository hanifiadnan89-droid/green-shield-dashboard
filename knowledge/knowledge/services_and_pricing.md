# Services and Pricing

Important rule: never invent pricing. If information is missing, ask for it or say it needs to be confirmed.

## Service abbreviations
- IQ = Insect Quarterly Home Protection
- RIT = Rodent Insect Triannual Home Protection
- TMM / T/M = Tick and Mosquito Monthly Protection
- BIT = Bed Bug Insect Triannual Home Protection
- OTS = One Time Service
- SPE = Residential Custom / maintenance or custom plan

## Insect Quarterly Home Protection (IQ)
Coverage: ants, spiders, wasps/hornets, general insects, foundation barrier, windows/doors/eaves, dusting, web/nest removal, interior treatment when needed.

Short explanation:
“We start by hitting the foundation hard with a high-end product that pests will take back to their nests. Instead of killing on contact, it works to keep pests out long-term. We then use a thirty-foot pole with a giant brush to knock down wasp nests and spider webs, and apply dust around eaves, windows, and doors to discourage future activity.”

Pricing:
- Regular initial: $375-$475
- Common recurring: $177 quarterly, explained as $59/month
- Older/common example: $375 initial and $165 quarterly, explained as $55/month
- Common current structure: $399 initial and $177 quarterly
- Minimum with approval: about $325 initial / $49 monthly
- Competitor match may go lower only with approval

Frequency: every 3 months. Explain that products last about 90 days, so returning quarterly keeps a barrier active and breaks the pest life cycle.

## Rodent Insect Triannual Home Protection (RIT)
Coverage: rodent baiting, interior/exterior tamper-resistant bait stations, sealing/exclusion, 1-month follow-up, every-4-month service after that, plus general insects/carpenter ants/wasps/spiders.

Short explanation:
“For rodents we have three steps. Step one is bait in tamper-resistant stations. Step two is sealing entry points inside and out with a product similar to steel wool. Step three, since rodents eat bugs, we also treat for ants, wasps, spiders, and other insects around the home.”

Pricing:
- Regular initial: $425-$525
- Common practical price: $449 initial
- Recurring: $65/month for 11 months
- 30-day follow-up included
- After follow-up, visits every 4 months
- Minimum with approval: about $349 initial / $55 monthly

## Tick and Mosquito Monthly Protection (TMM)
Coverage: perimeter, treeline, wooded edges, shrubs, bushes, landscaping, trees, lawn/foliage areas.

Short explanation:
“What separates us is that we are very thorough. We go about 15 feet up into the treeline for mosquitoes and about 15 feet out into wooded areas for ticks, while also hitting landscaping, bushes, trees, and high-activity zones.”

Pricing:
- Starts around $109 per treatment up to about 1/4 acre
- Common rates: $109, $119, $129, $139, $149, $159, $169 per treatment depending on treatment area
- Minimum when needed: $99 for many reasonably sized lots

Frequency: monthly April through September, sometimes May through September depending on start date.
Billing: charged per treatment after service.

## Bed Bug Insect Triannual Home Protection (BIT)
Requires prep sheet. Customer usually leaves for about 4 hours after treatment.

Pricing:
- Regular initial: $599-$699
- 14-day follow-up included
- Recurring: $65/month for 11 months
- Minimum with approval: about $549 initial / $55 monthly

Short explanation:
“We send a prep sheet first. When the technician arrives, they inspect and treat cracks, crevices, baseboards, mattresses, furniture, and nesting areas. You leave for about four hours while the product dries.”

## One-time service
One-time ant service: $449.
AI should usually recommend preventive ongoing protection when appropriate, because one-time work often does not break the egg/life cycle.

## Bundles and add-ons
TMM + Home Protection: always pursue if appropriate. Common discount: $100 off first combined service, can go to $150 if needed. Can also offer $10 off recurring TMM when needed.

Tick granulation: $30 per service or $10/month. Works best with IQ. Add red note visible to tech.

Rodent upgrade to new IQ: +$50 initial and +$6/month.
Existing IQ to RIT: often $349 initial and +$10/month. Create new agreement, schedule new IS, freeze old subscription.

Moles/voles: $70 upfront + $10/month. Moles are wildlife and not guaranteed. Voles can use exterior bait stations.

Rats: new RIT often $499 initial and $65/month. IQ upgrade for rats: $399 + $15/month, with fallback to regular rodent upgrade if needed to close.

Cockroaches: usually increase initial to $499. Send prep sheet. Customer exits about 4 hours.

Fleas: usually add $50-$100 to initial. Send prep sheet. Customer exits about 4 hours.

## Safety Wording (canonical source — all other files reference this)
Products are family and pet friendly once dry. Usually keep kids and pets away from treated areas until the product dries, which is about 30 minutes. Bed bugs, roaches, and fleas require the customer to leave for about 4 hours.

## Guarantee Wording
“We guarantee all of our work. If you ever have issues between visits, give us a call and we will return at no charge. We just ask that you are patient during the first month while we get things moving.”
