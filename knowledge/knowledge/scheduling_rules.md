# Scheduling Rules

Never invent availability. Use route finder or say availability needs to be checked.

## Timing
Same day: only if we will lose the sale and tech/scheduler approves.
Next day: discouraged and requires approval from Spencer/Rachel/Dana depending context.
Two days out: generally okay if placed correctly.

## Weekend rule
After 1 PM Saturday, do not alter Monday’s schedule.

## Window priority
Best to worst:
1. Anytime
2. Anytime with call-ahead
3. AM or PM
4. 4-hour window
5. 3-hour window
6. 2-hour window

Use 30-minute call-ahead to help customers accept larger windows.

## Tech home logic
Near tech home: earliest or latest window.
Far away: avoid bad early/late slots; often midday is better.
Avoid 10-2 windows because they often disrupt routing.

## Route map usage
Use FieldRoutes map to compare customer location with technician route order. Pay attention to route time and mileage.

## Outer areas
Some areas need specific routing days. Examples from training:
- Augusta / north of Lewiston-Auburn: Tuesday and Friday
- Poland / Casco: Wednesday
Use actual territory map/tool before confirming.

## Scheduler exceptions
Notify scheduler for deviations from Golden 4. Do not move Leo’s route or unassigned route without asking.

## Customer asks ETA
Give rough ETA based on tech route and ask tech to contact customer.

## Owner present rules
Quarterly/Rodent/Bed Bug initial services: owner should be home.
Tick/Mosquito: owner does not need to be home, but should send home/meter photo or be available by phone.
Landlord/out-of-town owner: welcome letter must be signed online before treatment.

## AI behavior
AI may ask:
“Do you prefer a morning or afternoon window?”
AI should not say a date/time is available unless tool confirms it.
